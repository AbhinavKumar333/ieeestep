About

The STEP program is an exciting program having the potential to have great impact on IEEE GOLD (Graduates of Last Decade) members and promises to provide necessary tools for the elevation from student member status to higher grade member status. GOLD is the IEEE entity which focuses on the needs of recent graduates and young professionals. Whether it’s pursuing post-graduate studies or becoming part of the workforce, GOLD provides special service focusing on both graduate student members (GSM) and full members respectively. Alongwith this staying actively involved in IEEE activities after graduation is key to a successful career and can help with sharpening professional development and technical skills, networking with top professionals in a chosen field and receiving the latest news on the cutting edge of technology. It is very important for graduating students to be introduced to the new local IEEE community—GOLD. This is where the STEP program comes in.




Significance of STEP

Members rely on IEEE as a source of technical and professional information, career resources and other benefits and services. To foster an interest in the engineering and technology profession, IEEE serves a vibrant student membership at universities and colleges around the world. Making the transition from student to young professional is often fraught with anxiety because of changes in location (moving to a first job or a new home) and changes in independence! The network of IEEE GOLD and young professional members found within local sections is key to helping make the transition a successful one.

Our Mission

The Student Elevation & Transition Partnership (STEP) program was created for local GOLD affinity groups to help facilitate the transition of student members as they graduate and become higher grade IEEE members. The program is intended to help introduce the opportunities and benefits of IEEE membership during the onset of a career.

Objectives

Recognize student members as they graduate and become part of the GOLD community Introduce graduating student to IEEE entity (GOLD) beyond the IEEE student branch Illustrate IEEE member benefits appropriate for young professional members Retain student members as they are elevated to higher grade IEEE members by communicating and demonstrating the value of IEEE membership for young professionals starting new careers

Events

Introductory Remarks

The STEP event should commence with a joint greeting and welcome speech by the Student Branch Chair and the local GOLD Chair. During these remarks the keynote speaker should be introduced, along with an overview of the event’s agenda.

10 minutes

Networking Social

Following the introduction, provide an opportunity for students to meet professionals, alumni and other guests. The students can update the guests with their coursework, thesis research and ongoing job search. Professionals have the opportunity to offer students valuable advice on state-of-the-art technologies in the field.

45 minutes

Keynote Speech

When the social period is completed, have the Student Branch Chair or the local GOLD Chair provide a brief introduction of the keynote speaker. Once the speaker is finished and any questions from the audience are responded to by the speaker, the Student Branch Chair or local GOLD Chair should give a closing thank you to the speaker.

10 minutes

Award Distribution

During this segment of the program, you have an opportunity to recognize the graduating students in attendance, any local or corporate sponsors and all the volunteers who organized and executed the program. If a contest was held prior to those invited, the winner should be announced and brought forward to receive the prize. Consider having pictures taken of the prize winner, speaker and key organizers and their teams.

15 minutes

Closing

The STEP event should conclude with closing remarks by the Student Branch Chair and the local GOLD Chair.

10 minutes
